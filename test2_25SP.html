1. Introduction and Data Wrangling¶

This dataset provides a comprehensive view of the amount of food waste each country in the world produces measured in both tonnes and kilograms per capita every year. It further classifies the amount of food waste into 3 separate categories, including household estimates, retail estimates, and food service estimates. These 3 categories have separate figures in kg/capita/year and tonnes/year.

Each row in this dataset represents a specific country in the World and their statistics concerning food waste. The rows are organized in alphabetical order. Specifically, the first letter of each country name organizes the data. Each column represents the features of a country's statistical data, as well as where the data came from.

In addition to the country's name and statistics, the countries are also categorized by their world region, which include Southeastern Asia, Southern Europe, Northern Africa, etc. In addition, each country is given a specific M49 code, which refers to the UN Standard Country (or Area Codes) for Statistical Use, a system developed by the United Nations Statistics Division for classifying and identifying countries and areas for statistical purposes. The M49 provides a unique (3-digit) numerical code for each country. Finally, the dataset provides confidence levels in these estimates, ranging from "Very Low Confidence" to "High Confidence".

In this report, I will use a regression model to predict if the categories that measure food waste in tonnes (household estimate, retail estimate, and food service estimate) as well as a country's region influence the total amount of food wasted (measured in kg/capita/year). I will also create a classification model to predict if a certain country falls under one of the following two regions: Sub-saharan Africa and Latin America and the Caribbean.
