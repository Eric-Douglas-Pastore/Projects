This network presents countries' alliances or enemies as a positive or negative relationship. Data from the correlates of war websites explores the positive and negative connections between many different countries (enemies and allies). Cycles (groups of countries) were based on their political, economic, and financial interactions etc. The more countries interacted with one another, the more likely they were grouped together. Groups of countries (cycles) consist of at least 3 or more countries.

This network studied whether or not a cycle contained an odd number of enemies (negative edges). After analyzing the data, it was determined that only 30% of the cycles contained an odd number of enemies. This means that the network is not structurally balanced. My calculations found that 1,136 cycles had an even number of negative edges, as opposed to the 490 cycles that had an odd number of negative edges. This tells me that even though the network is not 100% structurally balanced, it is not as imbalanced as one may think. Despite the imbalances, there are more cycles with an even number of negative edges than the contrary.

def neighborhood_overlap(g, u, v):
    n_common_nbrs = len(set(nx.common_neighbors(g, u, v)))
    n_join_nbrs = g.degree(u) + g.degree(v) - n_common_nbrs - 2
    return n_common_nbrs / n_join_nbrs
